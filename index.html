About Me, Myself, and I

Since you asked...

...after my apprenticeship as an Advertising Merchant and during my following studies in Media Science (with focus on computer science) I had quite a few and diverse jobs that broaden my horizon, to say the least:

Be it as Project manager, Conceptioner and Consultant in the field of Online and Strategic Marketing, Web Developer, Author of e-Learning Tutorials (CBT/WBT), Mobile phone tester for Vodafone (Acceptance & Terminal Testing), Student Trainee for WDR (Westdeutscher Rundfunk) in the nightshift - or as Intern for various Agencies that worked in Print, Webdesign, Advertising & Marketing...

But let's not forget my professesional, highly IT-relevant expertise I gained while I was working gloriously as „Stacker“ in Apple & Kiwifruit Packhouses and as „Thinner“ & „Picker“ on Apple Orchards in New Zealand, where I spent 1 year traveling around that awesome country (both the North & South Island) with a Stray-Bus Hop-On-Hop-Off-Ticket. I mean: There was this one incident, where I managed to talk the apple packhouse owner to let me use his one and only, most holy and sloooow internet connection (probably the only one on the south island), so that I could finally upload my first Rails-Project to the cloud (a photo blog for a university project). That guy even explained to me what a staging area was! But other jobs - like Stacker in a fish factory (hm) - didn't add any IT-value at all, I must admit (but we saw Dolphins during the shift!).

Neither did other jobs I had to perform during my studies in Germany, be it as Seller in a Bookstore, Packer in a screw factory, Newspaper Subscription Promoter, Moving Helper, Stacker in a warehouse, potato harvest helper - or as proffesional human cardboard shredder (like in: All day, by hand! Rooms full of cardboards in all sizes... That's where I got my Bruce Lee Tiger Claw...)

But those jobs teached me other stuff - like the Bruce Lee Tiger Claw f. i. ... that it's possible to sell 10 Books of the Shadowrun-Book-Series to one person ... or how to get over sore muscles ... and ... people skills of course!

Long story short (you still with me? Incredible!): After graduating in Media Studies I started my Doctorial Studies in the same field. But after I become the proud father of a wonderful son I had enough and dropped out to start a career as Web Developer. Which was a great decision, since I could join a neuefische Web Developer Bootcamp right away (after a short Corona bump in the road) to extend my slightly outdated knowledge in that field...
